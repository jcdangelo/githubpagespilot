Atherosclerotic Plaque

Microscopic view of a large elastic artery with atherosclerotic plaque within the intima. It is large enough to compromise the lumen and severely compress the muscular wall.  A special stain highlighting elastic tissue has been utilized.

Comments: Chief plaque components include smooth muscle cells admixed with macrophages and lymphocytes overlying a core of debris from necrotic smooth muscle cells and lipid-laden macrophages.
