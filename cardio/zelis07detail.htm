Interstitial Edema
(CHF - Kerley B)
Small black arrows (B) point to interlobular septa (Kerley B lines) which are visible due to interstitial edema or chronic hemosiderin deposits and fibrosis from chronic heart failure.  Do not confuse with fluid in the interlobar horizontal fissure, which is a much thicker line.  Notice prominence of bronchopulmonary linear markings from fluid in interstitial (peribronchial/perivascular) space.
