Atherosclerotic Plaque,
Cholesterol Clefts

Occlusive atherosclerotic plaque with cholesterol clefts, which are long, thin, tapered voids (arrows).  The clefts are empty (clear) because their lipid content is highly soluble in non-polar solvents like xylene used during histologic processing.  The lumen has been reduced to the small area at the asterix.
